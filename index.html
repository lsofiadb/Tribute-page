Charles Darwin

Charles Darwin was a British naturalist and biologist who lived during the 19th century. He is best known for his theory of evolution, which he developed during his travels and observations as a naturalist aboard the HMS Beagle. His groundbreaking book, "On the Origin of Species," introduced the concept of natural selection and revolutionized the way that scientists and the public alike understood the diversity of life on Earth. Darwin's work had a profound impact on the fields of biology, geology, and anthropology, and continues to influence scientific thought to this day.

Here's a time line of Charles Darwin's life

  • 1809 -Born in Shrewsbury.
  • 1825 -Enrolled in the University of Edinburgh to study medicine.
  • 1828 -Began studying theology at Cambridge.
  • 1830 -Botanist John Henslow sparks Darwin's interest in natural history.
  • 1831 -Embarks on a naturalist expedition aboard the Beagle.
  • 1837 -Moves to London.
  • 1839 -Publishes Voyage of the Beagle, a travelogue of his journey.
  • 1842 -First draft of the theory of evolution. Settles in Down.
  • 1858 -Joint presentation with A. R. Wallace before the Linnean Society of the theory of natural selection.
  • 1859 -Publishes On the Origin of Species.
  • 1862 -Publishes On the Various Contrivances by Which British and Foreign Orchids Are Fertilised by Insects.
  • 1871 -Publishes The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex.
  • 1877 -First Spanish translation of On the Origin of Species.
  • 1881 -Publishes The Formation of Vegetable Mould Through the Action of Worms.
  • 1882 -Dies in Down.
